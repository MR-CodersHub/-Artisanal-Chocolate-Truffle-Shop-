Our Vision & Philosophy

Sculpting Taste Into Pure Culinary Art

We began with a singular question: Can chocolate inspire the same intellectual complexity and emotional resonance as a fine painting or a rare vintage wine?

Cacao Artisanal was founded in 2018 as a direct response to the flavour uniformity of industrial chocolate. Our founders — chef Anelia Vance and cacao specialist Marcus Vance — spent two years travelling through South America and East Africa, documenting fermentation practices across 14 cooperatives before establishing direct-trade agreements with seven of them.

Every truffle and single-origin bar in our current collection is conched for 72 hours, hand-tempered on Carrara marble, and finished with natural cocoa butter tints in batches of 200 units or fewer. Each piece carries a flavour profile determined by its origin’s specific soil geochemistry, ambient fermentation temperature, and drying method — from the volcanic loam of Ecuador’s Guayas province to the humid canopy of Madagascar’s Sambirano Valley.

Core Tenets

Principles of Our Atelier

Every decision in our atelier is governed by three criteria: the provenance of raw materials, the purity of the composition, and the discipline of the hand-finish. These tenets define how we source, formulate, and release each production.

Micro-Lot Sourcing

We contract exclusively with single-family cooperatives in Ecuador, Madagascar, and Peru, limiting each harvest to parcels under 500 kilograms. This scale preserves the geochemical signature of each origin: the mineral composition of volcanic loam, the ambient humidity during fermentation, and the native yeast populations that drive flavour development.

Organic & Pure

Our sweetening protocol uses three ingredients only: organic unrefined cane sugar from Cauca Valley, wildflower honey harvested within 80 kilometres of the atelier, and caramel developed in copper kettles over a 6-hour thermal cycle. No invert syrups, no emulsifiers, no preservatives.

Artistic Presentation

Each truffle is hand-painted with cocoa butter tinted using natural mineral pigments: carmine, turmeric, and chlorophyll, applied in single-pass brushstrokes. Bars are cast in custom geometric moulds with beveled edges and debossed cacao percentages, eliminating the need for external packaging.

Sourcing & Import

Global Direct Trade Network

We maintain direct-trade relationships with seven cooperatives across five origin countries. Every import lot is accompanied by a provenance dossier: farm GPS coordinates, fermentation log, and FOB premium paid above the commodity market price.

Ethical Import Standards

Each shipment is governed by a direct-trade contract specifying a floor price 40% above the ICE futures market, with an additional quality premium paid upon cupping scores above 82 points. Cooperative audits are conducted twice annually by an independent third-party certifier.

Micro-Lot Export Network

Fermented beans are vacuum-sealed at origin within 12 hours of reaching the drying yard, then shipped in refrigerated containers held at 12°C to arrest spontaneous acid development during transit. Current origins: Ecuador (Nacional), Peru (Criollo), Madagascar (Trinitario), Vietnam (Trinitario hybrid).

Farm-to-Kitchen Traceability

Each finished product carries an alphanumeric lot code corresponding to a specific fermentation box at origin. Customers who enter this code on our platform receive the harvest date, drying method, producer name, and the corresponding SCA cupping score for that batch.

The Atelier Method

Production Protocol

Our method follows four sequential transformations: sourcing, roasting, conching, and finishing. Each is governed by specific time and temperature parameters developed through 400+ experimental batches since 2018.

01

Micro-Lot Sourcing

We source exclusively from farms that practise shade-grown agroforestry under native canopy, selecting ancient Nacional, Criollo, and Trinitario varieties with documented genetic lineage and documented SCA cupping scores above 80.

02

Artisanal Roasting

Beans are roasted in 5-kilogram batches within a drum roaster calibrated to a temperature curve peaking at 120°C. The profile is designed to preserve the citrus, floral, and herbaceous tasting notes specific to each lot’s fermentation method.

03

72-Hour Conching

Nibs are ground between pre-warmed granite melangeurs at 55 RPM for a minimum of 72 hours. This low-shear regimen reduces particle size below 25 microns while retaining volatile aromatic compounds that dissipate under high-friction industrial methods.

04

Hand-Painting & Tempering

Tempering follows a five-stage thermal curve executed on Carrara marble: melt to 45°C, cool to 27°C, warm to 31°C, hold for crystallisation, then deposit. Finished pieces are polished by hand with a dry chamois before packaging.

Production & Delivery

From Small Batches to Worldwide Reach

Batch sizes are capped at 200 units to preserve manual oversight at every stage. Our logistics network spans 32 countries through two temperature-controlled carrier agreements.

Micro-Batch Production

Each production run is limited to 200 units to enable individual inspection of every piece for gloss uniformity, snap acoustics, and fill-centre concentricity. Output is scheduled in 48-hour cycles with 24-hour resting periods between batches to allow equipment to return to ambient temperature.

Temperature-Controlled Logistics

Shipments are packed in expanded-polystyrene containers preconditioned to 16°C, with phase-change gel packs calibrated to maintain 16–20°C for 72 hours. Domestic transit: 24–48 hours via temperature-monitored courier. International: 3–5 business days to 32 countries under CITES-compliant customs documentation.

International Wholesale & Export

Our wholesale channel supplies 14 hospitality accounts across Europe and East Asia, including three Michelin-starred kitchens. We offer private-label formulation, custom cocoa butter colour matching, and subscription replenishment cycles aligned with each client’s menu rotation calendar.
